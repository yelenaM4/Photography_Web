You have master the basics of photography? Now it's time to move on to more advanced things.

Lets start by these 2 tips that will help you while taking portrait and landscape.

Portraits benefit from a wide aperture. When photographing people or animals, the subject should be the main focus of the photograph, and the easiest method to do this is to use a larger aperture. This will keep your topic crisp while blurring any surrounding distractions. Remember that a smaller f/ value indicates a larger aperture, and the larger the aperture, the more striking the impact. Some lenses can go as low as f/1.2, but even f/5.6 apertures will be enough. Switch to Aperture Priority Mode (Av or A) and experiment with different apertures to better understand how the aperture influences your photographs.

Landscape photography benefits with a narrow aperture. Landscape photography necessitates a unique approach, as everything from the foreground stone to the mountains in the background must be perfectly in focus. As a result, whenever you're shooting a subject where you want everything to be in focus, choose a small aperture rather than a wide one. A smaller aperture means a greater f/ number, so aim for f/22 or higher, depending on your lens's capabilities. Using Aperture Priority Mode (Av or A) will allow you to experiment with different apertures without having to alter the shutter speed every time.
